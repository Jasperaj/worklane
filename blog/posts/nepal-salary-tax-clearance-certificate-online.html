Salary Tax Clearance Certificate in Nepal — Online Process for Salaried Employees (2026)

By Upaj Manandhar, Chartered Accountant Jul 18, 2026 13 min read

The Salary Tax Clearance Certificate — कर चुक्ता प्रमाणपत्र (Kar Chukta Praman Patra) — is the single most-requested official confirmation that a salaried Nepali has paid their income tax for a fiscal year. Embassies want it before issuing a visa. Banks want it before disbursing a big loan. Universities abroad want it with your scholarship file. Government tender committees want it before you can bid. And thanks to the Inland Revenue Department's online Taxpayer Portal, if you are a salaried employee whose TDS was correctly withheld every month, you can obtain it in about 10 minutes without visiting a tax office. This guide walks you through the eligibility rules under Section 4(4) of the Income Tax Act, the prerequisites, the exact ird.gov.np pages you will see, the common reasons applications fail, and how to fix each one.

Key takeaways

  • Eligibility is narrow but common: a resident natural person whose only income is a single employment and whose TDS has been correctly withheld can obtain the certificate online under Section 4(4) of the Income Tax Act, 2058.
  • Prerequisites: personal PAN, active IRD Taxpayer Portal password, and Form 25-Ka (annual TDS certificate) from your employer — the last one is the most common blocker.
  • Portal path: ird.gov.np → Taxpayer Portal → login → General Services → Tax Clearance Certificate → select fiscal year → generate → download PDF.
  • No annual return needed for pure salary: Section 4(4) exempts pure-salary taxpayers with correct TDS from filing a full return. The STCC alone is the compliance proof.
  • Bank interest does not break Section 4(4). Personal bank interest carries 5% final withholding under Section 92; it is closed out at source and does not disqualify you from the fast online STCC path.

What the certificate actually is

The Tax Clearance Certificate is an IRD-issued document confirming that, for a specified fiscal year, all income tax dues have been settled. For a salaried person, "settled" means one specific thing: the employer withheld the right amount of TDS every month and deposited it to the IRD by the 25th of the following Nepali month (Section 90 of the Income Tax Act, 2058 — see our TDS guide for the wider framework).

The certificate carries your PAN, name, fiscal year, a machine-verifiable QR code and the IRD's digital signature. It is accepted by embassies, banks, universities, employers, and government tender committees throughout Nepal without the need for further attestation.

Who can apply — the Section 4(4) test

The online path is designed specifically for Section 4(4) taxpayers. Section 4(4) of the Income Tax Act, 2058 provides that a resident natural person is not required to file an annual return — and can obtain the STCC directly — if all of the following are true for the fiscal year:

  • Only income during the year is employment income (salary);
  • Employment is from a single employer (or, in some interpretations, employers whose payroll accurately combined for TDS);
  • The employer has correctly withheld TDS every month and issued Form 25-Ka;
  • No business, rental, capital gain, foreign, or investment income above what "final withholding" already closes out.

Bank interest on a personal deposit and dividend from Nepali companies are subject to 5% final withholding under Section 92 and do not force you out of Section 4(4). Similarly, windfall / lottery gains above the exemption limit are separately closed at 25% final withholding. The test is whether you have any income that would require a full annual return — active business income, rent from your own property to an entity, capital gain on shares — not whether you had any receipts at all.

Prerequisites

Before you sit down at the portal, gather:

  1. Your personal PAN (9-digit). Personal, not your employer's. If you have never had a personal PAN, register at your local IRD office or via ird.gov.np before proceeding.
  2. Active IRD Taxpayer Portal username and password. The username is normally your PAN. If you never set a password, use the "Forgot Password" flow with the email/phone registered against your PAN.
  3. Form 25-Ka from your employer for the target fiscal year. Your employer is required to issue this within 15 days of the end of Ashadh (year-end) under Section 90 of the Income Tax Act. If they have not, ask — it is a legal obligation, not a favour.
  4. A quiet 10 minutes and a browser (Chrome, Edge or Firefox). Mobile works but is fiddly for the download step.

Step-by-step online process

The full online workflow — from login to downloaded PDF

Steps 1–3 are your inputs; steps 4–6 are the portal's automated response once the data matches.

The detailed steps:

  1. Go to ird.gov.np. On the homepage, click the Taxpayer Portal button (top-right).
  2. Log in using your PAN as username and your portal password. If prompted, complete the OTP sent to your registered mobile / email.
  3. Navigate to the STCC service. From the left-hand navigation, choose General Services → Tax Clearance Certificate (कर चुक्ता प्रमाणपत्र).
  4. Select the fiscal year for which you need the certificate. The dropdown lists years for which the IRD has your assessment closed.
  5. Review the auto-filled details. The page shows your name, PAN, employer PAN, total salary, and total TDS as recorded from the employer's monthly returns. Compare against your Form 25-Ka. If numbers match, proceed.
  6. Submit. The system runs an automated check: employer's monthly TDS deposits vs your salary, absence of arrears, and Section 4(4) eligibility.
  7. Download the PDF. If everything reconciles, the certificate is generated instantly. Download the PDF (bearing IRD digital signature and QR code) to your device.

What the certificate looks like

The generated PDF has a fixed layout:

  • Header: Government of Nepal / Ministry of Finance / Inland Revenue Department, with the IRD logo.
  • Title: "Tax Clearance Certificate" in English and कर चुक्ता प्रमाणपत्र in Nepali.
  • Body: PAN, name, fiscal year, issue date, and a statement that "all income tax dues for the fiscal year have been fully settled."
  • Verification: a QR code linking to the IRD's certificate verification portal, and a digital signature.

Anyone can verify the certificate by scanning the QR code or entering the certificate number at ird.gov.np → General Services → Certificate Verification. That is how embassies confirm authenticity.

The QR code is the trust anchor. A photocopy or scanned image works as well as the original — the embassy is verifying the certificate, not the paper.

The most common single blocker in our experience is the third row: the employer's total in the monthly e-TDS returns does not match the annual Form 25-Ka they hand you. Reconcile before applying — it saves both sides a call to the IRD.

Where you will actually use it

  • Embassy / visa applications — most common single reason. Schengen, US, UK, Australian and Japanese embassies routinely list Tax Clearance Certificate as required documentation.
  • Foreign university admission & scholarship — required in the financial documentation packet.
  • Bank loans — home loans, education loans, and personal loans above certain limits typically require an STCC or the last three years' returns as an alternative.
  • Government tender / bidding — companies and consultants participating in Government of Nepal tenders must submit an STCC.
  • Public-sector job applications — many recruiting boards require it at the interview stage.
  • Property registration (specific municipalities) — some Ward offices ask for it before permitting transfer registration above threshold values.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to file an annual return before applying for STCC?

If you meet Section 4(4) (only salary, correctly withheld), no. Section 4(4) explicitly exempts you from filing D-01. If you have any other income (rent, business, capital gains), yes — file D-01 first and apply for STCC on completion of assessment.

Is there a fee for the STCC?

Currently, the online Tax Clearance Certificate is issued free of cost. There is no e-payment step during the application.

Can I get an STCC on the same day I quit a job?

You can apply for the STCC of a fiscal year only after that year has ended and the employer has filed the annual e-TDS return. In practical terms, an STCC for FY 2082/83 is available from mid-August 2026 onwards once your employer completes their annual filing.

What if my employer will not issue Form 25-Ka?

Section 90 of the Income Tax Act requires the employer to issue Form 25-Ka within 15 days of the fiscal-year end. Failure is a legal breach. Escalate in writing; if still refused, file a complaint at your Inland Revenue Office — the employer's PAN will be flagged, which usually resolves the issue quickly.

Can a non-resident salaried employee use the online STCC?

Yes, provided all income during the year is Nepali-source employment income taxed at the non-resident rate and withholding was correctly done. Non-residents with foreign income should consult a CA — dual-taxation treaty situations often need a separate D-01 filing.

What is the difference between STCC and Form 25-Ka?

Form 25-Ka is the employer's certificate of TDS deducted — it is the source document. STCC is the IRD's certificate that all tax dues for the year are settled — it is the compliance proof. Embassies and banks want the STCC; internal audit and personal records use Form 25-Ka.

Getting help

Most salaried applications go through the portal in one sitting. The one that stalls almost always stalls because of an employer-side TDS mismatch — the employer's e-TDS return does not tie to your Form 25-Ka. A quick call from a CA to the employer's finance team usually clears it in a day.
