TDS Rates in Nepal FY 2082/83: Complete Rate Chart, Deadlines and Compliance Guide

By Upaj Manandhar, Chartered Accountant Jul 1, 2026 · Updated Jul 15, 2026 13 min read

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) is the mechanism by which the payer withholds tax before paying you and deposits it with the Inland Revenue Department (IRD) on your behalf. It is governed by Sections 87 – 92 of Nepal's Income Tax Act, 2058, and the rate depends on the nature of the payment and the status of the payee. Getting it wrong is expensive: the deductor is personally liable for the full tax plus interest and fees, whether or not the recipient later files a return.

Key takeaways

  • TDS is deducted on the amount excluding VAT — a common source of over- or under-withholding.
  • Deposit is due within 25 days of the end of the Nepali month; late deposit attracts 15% p.a. interest plus fees.
  • Services from VAT-registered vendors attract 1.5%; services from PAN-only vendors attract 15% — the gap is designed to reward formal VAT registration.
  • "Final" withholdings on dividends, bank interest and windfall gains close the tax event — no further return on that income.
  • e-TDS filing through the IRD portal is mandatory for most withholders; Form 25-Ka certificates are generated after filing.

Sections you should know

  • Section 87 — TDS on employment income (salary).
  • Section 88 — General withholding (services, contracts, rent, royalty).
  • Section 88Ka — Withholding on gain from investment insurance and interest.
  • Section 89 — Withholding on contract payments and consumer committee payments.
  • Section 90 — Deposit of tax withheld (25 days).
  • Section 92 — Final withholding tax (dividend, bank interest, windfall).

Deposit deadlines and penalties

  • Monthly deposit: within 25 days after the end of each Nepali month (Section 90). Example: Shrawan TDS due by 25 Bhadra.
  • Interest on late deposit: 15% per annum from due date to deposit date (Section 119).
  • Fee for late filing: 1.5% per annum of the tax amount, plus flat fees.
  • Penalty for wilful default: up to 100% of the shortfall.
  • Annual reconciliation: the withholder files an annual TDS return; Form 25-Ka certificates must be issued to each deductee within 15 days of fiscal year-end.

The rules that catch people out

  • TDS is on value excluding VAT. "Services NPR 1,00,000 + 13% VAT" → TDS at 1.5% is NPR 1,500, not NPR 1,695.
  • VAT-registered vs PAN-only gap is deliberate. If a vendor gives you a PAN-only invoice, you must withhold 15% unless a specific exemption applies.
  • The NPR 50,000 threshold is cumulative per vendor per year. Once total payments to one vendor cross NPR 50,000, TDS applies to subsequent payments.
  • Individual house rent is a special case. Rent paid to an individual landlord for a residential house is outside the Income Tax Act's TDS regime — it is taxed at the local body level (municipal house-rent tax). Rent paid to an entity, or non-residential rent, follows the standard 10%.
  • Reimbursements can be tricky. Pure reimbursement of expenses (with vendor invoices in the client's name) is generally outside TDS. A "consolidated" service fee that bundles reimbursement is not.

Why TDS actually benefits taxpayers

  • No year-end tax shock. Tax is collected in monthly slices instead of a lump sum at return time.
  • Credit against final liability. Every rupee withheld offsets your annual tax; excess TDS is refundable on filing.
  • "Final" withholdings close the matter. Dividends, bank interest and windfall gains need no further return for that income.
  • Proof of income. Form 25-Ka certificates are widely accepted by banks and embassies as verified income records for loans and visa applications.
  • Compliance signal for businesses. Clean, timely TDS is the cheapest audit insurance — it is the first thing the IRD checks when it opens a file.

e-TDS — filing through the IRD portal

  1. Log in to ird.gov.np with your business PAN credentials.
  2. Prepare the monthly TDS statement (Excel template from the portal) with each deductee's PAN, section, gross, and TDS.
  3. Upload, review, and generate the payment voucher.
  4. Pay via authorised bank or online payment gateway before the 25th of the following month.
  5. Issue Form 25-Ka certificates to deductees.

Frequently asked questions

Do individuals need to deduct TDS?

Individuals generally do not withhold on personal payments. However, an individual who pays salaries (household staff, staff of a personal business), or rent to an entity, or professional fees for business purposes, may be required to withhold.

What if a vendor refuses to accept TDS deduction?

The obligation is on the payer, not the payee. A vendor cannot "opt out" of TDS — a "grossed-up" agreement (payer bears TDS) is common where market power allows, but the payer still deposits the tax.

Can excess TDS be refunded?

Yes — where withheld TDS exceeds final assessed liability, the excess is refundable on filing the annual return. In practice, adjustment against subsequent liability is faster than a cash refund.

Is separate TDS registration required?

No separate registration — the same business PAN is used to file TDS returns.

Getting your TDS ledger reviewed

If you pay rent, salaries, or vendor fees and are not certain your TDS ledger is correct — or you have received a TDS assessment notice from the IRD — a Chartered Accountant can review it before the tax office does. In most cases we find at least one recoverable historical over-deduction.
