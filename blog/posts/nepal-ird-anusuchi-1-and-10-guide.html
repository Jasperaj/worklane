Nepal IRD Portal — Anusuchi 1 (Tax Computation) and Anusuchi 10 (Annual TDS Return): Field-by-Field Guide

By Upaj Manandhar, Chartered Accountant Jul 18, 2026 17 min read

If our STCC guide was the express lane for salaried Section 4(4) taxpayers, this post is for everyone else: the individual who has to file a full D-01 return with Anusuchi 1, and the employer who has to file the Anusuchi 10 annual TDS return. Both live on ird.gov.np. Both have field-by-field rules that determine whether the portal accepts your submission or bounces it. And both can be auto-populated from data you already have — if you know where the buttons are. This guide walks through every mandatory and optional field, shows how the tax calculation waterfalls through Anusuchi 1, and lays out the three auto-populate paths for Anusuchi 10 side-by-side.

Key takeaways

  • Anusuchi 1 is a waterfall: Assessable Income → Statutory Deductions → Taxable Income → Slab Tax → Rebates → TDS Credit → Net Payable / Refundable. Every arrow is a formula the portal runs; every input is a field you fill.
  • On Anusuchi 1, the six mandatory fields are: PAN, Fiscal Year, Filing Status, Employment Income, Deductions block (zero-fill if none), and TDS Credit. Business, rent and capital-gain sections are conditional and open only if you tick the relevant box.
  • Anusuchi 10 is the annual employer return — one row per employee with PAN, name, months worked, gross salary and TDS, and it reconciles with all 12 monthly e-TDS filings.
  • Three ways to auto-populate Anusuchi 10: (1) IRD Excel template offline fill + upload; (2) portal's "Import from Monthly Returns" button; (3) manual employee-by-employee entry. Method 2 is fastest and safest.
  • The submission gate for both schedules is reconciliation: for Anusuchi 1, portal-recorded TDS must equal your Form 25-Ka credit claim; for Anusuchi 10, the annual totals must equal the sum of 12 monthly returns.

Anusuchi 1 vs Anusuchi 10 — who files what

Nepal's Income Tax Act, 2058 organises tax schedules with Nepali numerals. Two are far more visible than the rest:

ScheduleWho filesWhenWhere in the portal
Anusuchi 1 (Schedule 1) — Tax rate schedule / Individual computationA natural person (individual) who has to file a return — mixed income, multiple employers, capital gain, business, etc.Within 3 months of fiscal year-end (Ashwin end); extendable by another 3 monthsReturn Filing → Income Tax → D-01 (Individual)
Anusuchi 10 (Schedule 10) — Annual TDS return of the employerAny employer who withheld TDS on salary during the yearWithin 3 months of fiscal year-end (Ashwin end)Return Filing → TDS → Annual Return (Anusuchi 10)

Note the split: Anusuchi 1 is filed under your personal PAN; Anusuchi 10 is filed under the employer's business PAN. If you are the accountant of a company, you may be logged into both portals in the same session.

Where they sit in the portal navigation

Getting to Anusuchi 1 and Anusuchi 10 from the Taxpayer Portal home

Same login page, two different PANs, two different filing paths. Do not try to file Anusuchi 10 under a personal PAN or Anusuchi 1 under an employer PAN — the portal simply will not show the option.

Anusuchi 1 — page-by-page

The D-01 individual return has five pages. Anusuchi 1 spans the middle three:

  1. Page 1 — General Information. PAN, Name, Address, Fiscal Year, Filing Status, contact info.
  2. Page 2 — Income Details (Anusuchi 1 begins). Employment income, business income (if any), rental income (if any), investment income (if any), capital gain (if any).
  3. Page 3 — Deductions. SSF/EPF/CIT, life insurance, health insurance, remote area, disabled concession, female individual rebate flag.
  4. Page 4 — Tax Computation. Auto-generated waterfall from Pages 2 & 3. Read-only.
  5. Page 5 — TDS Credit & Payment. TDS deducted at source (auto-pulled), self-assessment payment, refund / additional payment.

The portal validates the deduction inputs against the annual gross salary from Page 2 — you cannot claim a NPR 60,000 life insurance premium if your salary was NPR 2,00,000, because the cap is the lower of actual premium or NPR 40,000.

Worked example — a NPR 12,00,000 salary

Same numbers as our salary slab post, run through the Anusuchi 1 fields for FY 2082/83:

  1. Page 2 — Income: Employer PAN 601XXXXXX, ABC Pvt Ltd, Gross Salary 12,00,000.
  2. Page 3 — Deductions: SSF Employee 1,32,000; Life Insurance 40,000; Others zero.
  3. Page 4 — Auto compute:
    • Taxable Income: 12,00,000 − 1,32,000 − 40,000 = NPR 10,28,000.
    • Slab tax (SSF contributor, so 0% on first 5 lakh): 10% × 2L + 20% × 3L + 30% × 28,000 = NPR 88,400.
    • If female + individual: Female rebate 10% = 8,840; Net tax = NPR 79,560.
  4. Page 5 — TDS Credit: auto-pulled from employer's monthly returns. If TDS deducted & deposited matched net tax, portal shows Refund / Payable = 0. If more was deducted than owed, portal shows Refund; if less, portal shows Additional Payable.
The waterfall is deterministic. If the auto-computed tax on Page 4 does not match what you expected, the bug is on Page 2 or Page 3 — not the slab formula.

Anusuchi 1 field map — one glance

Fill the mandatory column completely. Only tick a box in the optional column if you have the underlying transaction — an unopened box saves you an entire schedule.

Anusuchi 10 — page-by-page

Anusuchi 10 is the annual TDS return the employer files after the fiscal year ends. Structurally it is simpler than D-01 but wider — one row per employee. Pages:

  1. Page 1 — Header. Business PAN, business name, fiscal year, total number of employees, total gross salary, total TDS deducted.
  2. Page 2 — Employee Grid. A grid with one row per employee. Columns detailed below.
  3. Page 3 — Reconciliation. Auto-shows monthly deposits vs annual total. Flags mismatches.
  4. Page 4 — Submit. Digital signature (or logged-in PAN acknowledgment), submit, download acknowledgment.

Three ways to auto-populate Anusuchi 10

Three routes to fill Anusuchi 10

Method 2 is our default recommendation for anyone who has been filing monthly returns anyway — one click, guaranteed reconciliation.

Method 1 — the IRD Excel template

  1. On the Anusuchi 10 page, click Download Template. An .xlsx file downloads with two sheets: Instructions and Employees.
  2. Open the file, keep the header row exactly as is. Do not insert or delete columns.
  3. Fill one row per employee. Use ISO date format (YYYY-MM-DD) or the Nepali BS date the portal expects (varies by portal version; the instruction sheet tells you which).
  4. Save and re-upload via Upload Template on the same page.
  5. Portal parses the file, shows a preview of accepted rows and a list of rejected rows with the reason. Common rejects: invalid PAN, name mismatch, negative TDS.
  6. Fix the rejected rows in Excel, re-upload, or accept the partial and complete the balance manually.

If you already run payroll from a well-designed Excel workbook (see our Excel HR/payroll guide), you can map your Master + Payroll sheets to the IRD template columns with an XLOOKUP or INDEX/MATCH — Method 1 becomes a copy-paste job.

Method 2 — import from monthly returns

The best-kept-secret button on the portal:

  1. On the Anusuchi 10 header page (Page 1), click Import from Monthly Returns.
  2. The portal reads all 12 monthly e-TDS returns you filed under the same PAN and fiscal year.
  3. It aggregates per employee: sums gross salary and TDS across the months, counts months worked.
  4. You see a pre-filled Employee Grid on Page 2 — nothing typed manually.
  5. Review, edit if needed (rare — usually only if an employee joined/left mid-year and monthly returns need a manual correction), and proceed to Page 3.
  6. Page 3 automatically reconciles because the source data is the same monthly returns.

The Import method inherits whatever accuracy exists in your monthly filings. Garbage monthly returns → garbage Anusuchi 10. Correct monthly returns → clean Anusuchi 10 in one click.

Method 3 — manual entry

Add row by row in the portal grid. Fine for a business with 2–3 employees on payroll. Beyond that, the risk of a typo forcing a Page 3 reconciliation failure grows fast. If you must go manual, follow one rule: enter one employee, save draft, verify the running total, then add the next. Do not batch-enter and save at the end — the portal's session-save behaviour is not generous with unsaved work.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use the same Excel template across fiscal years?

No — the IRD updates the template each year (small formatting changes, sometimes new mandatory columns like SSF flag). Download a fresh template each fiscal year to avoid rejection.

If I use "Import from Monthly Returns", do I still need Form 25-Ka?

Yes. Form 25-Ka is the certificate you issue to each employee — the portal does not print it for you. Generate 25-Ka from your payroll or an Excel template after Anusuchi 10 is filed. It must be issued within 15 days of fiscal year end (Section 90).

Can Anusuchi 10 be revised?

Yes. Filed Anusuchi 10 can be revised through the portal — click Revise Return on the filed acknowledgement. Interest under Section 119 applies if the revision increases the deposit; refunds are adjusted against future deposits.

What if an employee's PAN was inactive during part of the year?

Their row still enters Anusuchi 10 with the actual months worked. Portal accepts it but flags "PAN inactive." The employee's own return will reconcile once they reactivate PAN. Do not skip inactive-PAN employees — TDS deducted must still be reported.

Is Anusuchi 10 different from the monthly e-TDS returns?

Yes. The 12 monthly returns cover only salary TDS deposit per month. Anusuchi 10 is the annual reconciliation — same data, aggregated per employee for the year, plus deductions considered by the employer and the annual tax estimate.

Does the employee need to file anything after Anusuchi 10 is filed?

If the employee is a Section 4(4) case (single employer, correct TDS, only-salary), they do not file a return — they just apply for the Salary Tax Clearance Certificate after the employer files Anusuchi 10.

Getting help

Most Anusuchi 10 rejections trace back to an unfixed monthly return. Fix the source, re-import, and the annual return closes. Most Anusuchi 1 rejections trace back to an employer-side deposit gap or a name-spelling mismatch. Fix at the employer level, then re-attempt on the personal PAN.
