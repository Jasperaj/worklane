Nepal Salary Tax Slabs FY 2082/83 (2025/26): Full Table, Deductions and the FY 2083/84 Overhaul

By Upaj Manandhar, Chartered Accountant Jul 2, 2026 · Updated Jul 15, 2026 12 min read

Nepal taxes salaries on a progressive slab basis: each rate applies only to the portion of income inside that band, never to your whole salary. This is the single most common misunderstanding among salaried employees — and it matters, because it changes how promotions and bonuses translate into take-home pay. Here is the complete structure for FY 2082/83 (Shrawan 2082 – Ashadh 2083, mid-July 2025 – mid-July 2026), set by the Finance Act 2082, along with a worked example and a preview of the major overhaul announced for FY 2083/84.

Key takeaways

  • The 1% first slab is a Social Security Tax (SST) — fully waived for SSF contributors, so most private-sector employees pay 0% on the first NPR 5 lakh.
  • Couple filing widens the 1% band from NPR 5 lakh to NPR 6 lakh — but a married woman with only salary income usually still saves more by filing as an individual and taking the 10% female rebate.
  • Statutory deductions (SSF + EPF + CIT + insurance premiums) apply before the slabs and can materially lower taxable income.
  • The Finance Bill 2083 introduces a unified slab schedule from FY 2083/84 — the widest first band in decades, with a 29% top rate.

FY 2082/83 salary tax slabs — individual (unmarried or married-individual)

Taxable income (NPR)Rate on the slab
Up to 5,00,0001% Social Security Tax*
5,00,001 – 7,00,00010%
7,00,001 – 10,00,00020%
10,00,001 – 20,00,00030%
20,00,001 – 50,00,00036% (30% + 20% surcharge)
Above 50,00,00039% (30% + 30% surcharge)

*The 1% SST is waived entirely for SSF contributors and for taxpayers whose only income is pension income.

Couple filing is optional and revocable each year — see our post on the 10% female rebate for when couple vs individual filing wins.

FY 2082/83 individual slab — marginal rate by income band

Marginal rates apply only within each band. The 1% first slab is waived for SSF contributors.

Deductions that apply before the slabs

  • SSF + EPF + CIT contributions — combined cap of NPR 5,00,000 or one-third of assessable income, whichever is lower.
  • Life insurance premium — actual premium paid, up to NPR 40,000.
  • Health insurance premium — actual premium paid, up to NPR 20,000.
  • Remote-area allowance — up to NPR 50,000 depending on area category (A/B/C/D/E).
  • Female individual rebate — 10% of the computed tax for a resident woman with only employment income filing individually.
  • Disabled person concession — 50% of the basic exemption threshold, added to the basic exemption.

Worked example — a NPR 12,00,000 salary

Assume gross annual salary NPR 12,00,000, SSF contribution NPR 1,32,000 (11% employee), life insurance NPR 40,000, no other deductions. Filing as an individual.

  • Taxable income after deductions: 12,00,000 − 1,32,000 − 40,000 = NPR 10,28,000.
  • Tax: SSF contributor so 0% on first 5,00,000 + 10% × 2,00,000 (20,000) + 20% × 3,00,000 (60,000) + 30% × 28,000 (8,400) = NPR 88,400.
  • If the employee is a resident woman, apply the 10% rebate: NPR 79,560.

Effective tax rate: 79,560 / 10,28,000 ≈ 7.7% — a useful counter to the "I'm in the 30% bracket, I pay 30%" fallacy.

What is coming in FY 2083/84 — the unified schedule

The budget speech of Jestha 15, 2083 (May 29, 2026) announced the largest personal tax reset in years, effective Shrawan 1, 2083 (mid-July 2026). Under the Finance Bill 2083, salary income for all individuals (individual and couple alike) is proposed to be taxed as:

Taxable income (NPR)Rate
Up to 10,00,0001%
10,00,001 – 15,00,00010%
15,00,001 – 25,00,00020%
25,00,001 – 40,00,00027%
Above 40,00,00029%

The separate couple slab disappears — the first band is already NPR 10 lakh for everyone. The top rate falls from 39% to 29%, and the whole schedule flattens. If passed as gazetted, a salaried person earning NPR 10 lakh a year would move from three tax bands down to a single 1% band.

What you should do now

  • Payroll for Shrawan 2082 – Ashadh 2083 continues to follow the FY 2082/83 table above.
  • From Shrawan 1, 2083 (mid-July 2026), payroll should switch to the new schedule — verify your first Shrawan payslip reflects it.
  • Confirm final figures against the gazetted Finance Act 2083 and subsequent IRD circulars — proposed and enacted figures sometimes differ slightly.
  • Review your deduction stack (insurance, SSF/EPF) each year — small increases can shift you across a slab boundary.

Frequently asked questions

How is TDS on salary calculated?

The employer estimates full-year taxable salary, applies deductions, computes tax using the slab schedule, and divides by 12. Monthly TDS is adjusted for bonuses and changes as the year progresses. See our post on TDS in Nepal for the wider TDS framework.

Do I need to file a personal return if TDS was correctly deducted?

Individuals with income only from employment and correctly-deducted TDS are generally not required to file an annual return under Section 4(4) of the Income Tax Act. Filing voluntarily is still useful for documenting income (loans, visas).

What income is exempt from salary tax?

Common exemptions: employer's PF/CIT contributions up to the cap, medical treatment reimbursements against bills up to specified limits, uniforms and tools required for the job, death and gratuity payments up to prescribed limits.

Does the female rebate still apply under the FY 2083/84 proposal?

The Finance Bill 2083 retains the 10% female rebate for individual-filing resident women with only employment income. Because the underlying tax base falls sharply, the absolute saving from the rebate also falls — but the rebate itself survives.
