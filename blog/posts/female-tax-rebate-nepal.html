Nepal's 10% Female Tax Rebate (FY 2082/83): Married vs Unmarried Women — Which Filing Status Wins?

By Upaj Manandhar, Chartered Accountant Jul 9, 2026 · Updated Jul 15, 2026 9 min read

Nepal's Income Tax Act, 2058 gives resident women a meaningful concession: a 10% rebate on the computed income tax liability (Schedule 1, paragraph on natural persons). It is straightforward on paper — and confusing in practice, because the rebate depends on how you file rather than only on your gender. This guide explains who qualifies, why married women filing jointly lose it, and shows the actual rupee difference between individual and couple filing at common salary levels for FY 2082/83.

Key takeaways

  • The 10% rebate is applied to the tax liability, not to income — so its rupee value scales with your salary.
  • It is available only to a resident natural woman assessed as an individual whose taxable income is only from employment in Nepal.
  • Choosing couple status disqualifies the rebate — that is the single biggest reason married women lose it.
  • Break-even between "wider couple slab" and "10% rebate" is around NPR 7–8 lakh of taxable salary.
  • Marital status for tax is an opt-in choice each year — you can revisit it every filing.

Who qualifies for the rebate

The rebate applies where all three conditions are met:

  • The taxpayer is a resident natural person (in Nepal > 183 days in a fiscal year, or by treaty rules).
  • The person is a woman.
  • Taxable income is only from employment (remuneration) in Nepal and she is assessed as an individual.

The rebate is calculated after all statutory deductions (SSF/EPF/CIT, life insurance, health insurance) — 10% is knocked off the final tax figure, not off the salary.

Why married women often lose it — filing status vs marital status

Two words matter here: individual and couple. The Income Tax Act lets a married couple be assessed either separately (each spouse as an individual with their own PAN and return) or jointly (as a "couple" — one combined return). The choice is annual and voluntary.

  • Unmarried women can only file individually — so the rebate applies automatically if income is only from employment.
  • Married women who opt for couple filing lose the rebate — because the "couple" is a distinct assessee, not a natural woman, and the concession is denied to couple-status assessees.
  • Married women who file individually under their own PAN retain the rebate, provided their income is solely from employment.
A married woman does not lose the rebate by being married — she loses it by choosing couple filing status.

Couple slab or 10% rebate — worked examples (FY 2082/83)

Individual slabs: 1% first 5 lakh (waived for SSF), 10% next 2 lakh, 20% next 3 lakh, 30% next 10 lakh, 36% next 30 lakh, 39% above. Couple filing widens the first 1% slab to NPR 6 lakh (then 6–8 lakh at 10%, 8–11 lakh at 20%). Assumes no SSF (1% chargeable) and no other deductions.

The break-even is around NPR 7–8 lakh of taxable salary. Below that, the wider couple slab wins; above it, the 10% rebate wins. And critically, if the spouse also earns, couple filing usually loses again, because the couple slab is applied once to combined income and the extra income is pushed into higher bands.

Deductions that apply before the slabs

  • SSF + EPF + CIT contributions — combined cap of NPR 5,00,000 or one-third of assessable income, whichever is lower.
  • Life insurance premium — actual premium up to NPR 40,000.
  • Health insurance premium — actual premium up to NPR 20,000.
  • Remote-area allowance — up to NPR 50,000 (varies by area category).

The 10% female rebate then applies to the tax computed after the above deductions.

Practical points employers and employees miss

  • Your employer must factor the rebate into monthly TDS — check your payslip; salaried women often see slightly lower TDS than male peers on the same gross.
  • The rebate covers employment income only. Any business or investment income disqualifies it — a woman running a boutique alongside her job cannot claim it.
  • Filing status can be changed each year; you are not locked in.
  • From FY 2083/84 the slab structure changes significantly under the Finance Act 2083 (see our salary tax slab post). The Finance Bill 2083 retains the 10% female rebate, but confirm final wording in the gazetted Act.

Frequently asked questions

Does the rebate apply to non-resident women earning in Nepal?

No — the rebate is available only to resident natural women. Non-residents are taxed at the flat non-resident rate on Nepal-source income.

Can a woman with both salary and rental income claim the rebate?

No. The rebate is restricted to a woman whose only income is from employment. Adding any other head of income (rent, business, capital gains) disqualifies it.

How is the rebate shown in the tax return?

In the annual return (D-01 for individuals), the tax is computed under Schedule 1 and the 10% concession is applied at the "Female Rebate" line before arriving at net tax payable. Payroll systems normally handle this automatically once the employee is flagged as female-individual on the master.
